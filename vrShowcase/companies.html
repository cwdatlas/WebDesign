Companies That Produce VR products

There are many companies that are in the VR space, but few that make VR headsets and fiewer that have a large market share. The VR headset market is a near monopoly with Oculus/Meta having the vest majority of market share. This topic is a hot one, so here is Thrill to give you an introduction to it and what the VR community can do to make sure that they keep their privacy and independence from Meta.

Oculus/Meta

Oculus, the maker of the Oculus Quest and Quest2 and owned by Meta. If you arent up to date Facebook changed their name to Meta. Meta has the lionshare of the market share putting them in teritory of a near monopoly esspecially with its appstore and how a person needs a facebook account to use their Quest. If you watched the video above you would have found out the general atitude that the VR community has in relation to Meta. Buy a quest if you dont have any other options, but make sure to keep up to date so you can help in the fight against Meta if needed.

Valve

Valve is a far more friendly company than Meta, but still is monolithic with the second largest in market share in VR headsets. That market share is a small fraction of Meta's, but it makes up the more enthusiest community as their headset, yes singular, is one of the best headsets in the market aside from exceptionally expensive headsets. They also have the largest game store on PCs, which is where you would likely go to play any of your games in SteamVR, their app store's VR launcher.

htc

htc has been in the tech industry for a long time. Before they made headsets they made phones back before 2010. They sold that section of the company and pivitted to VR with their first headset being the htc Vive, my first and primary headset lauched back in 2015. Today they are a mildy successful company in the consumer VR headset market, and more successful in the business market.

Pimax

Pimax is the exentric VR headset producer. These headsets bost high field of view and high resolutions. Pimax produces many headsets that are primarily catigorized with their resolution. The field of view often results in distoration on the sides of the screen.
